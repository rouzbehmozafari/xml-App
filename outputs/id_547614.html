We are currently seeking a full-time an (Associate) Scientist Clinical Data Analysis/ Statistical Programming*. You will work in T&amp;uuml;bingen, in Munich or home based (Germany). Your mission

Your main responsibility will be the analysis of patient data from our Phase 1 clinical trials, such as patient characteristics, adverse events, response rates or survival. This will include:

  • Statistical Programming:
    • Use data exports from clinical electronic data capture (EDC) systems/ electronic case report forms (eCRF) and additional biomarker data from multiple sources to generate statistical outputs (tables, figures, listings [TFLs]) using SAS programming. Those will be required for data reporting and reconciliation, and to answer clinical or scientific questions to support the development of our immunotherapies
    • Prepare graphs and figures for the discussion of results in cross-functional teams
    • Work closely together with clinical data management to ensure accurate and complete data collection in the clinical EDC systems
    • Contribute to improving workflows as well as implementing and executing quality measures to ensure data integrity
  • Biostatistics:
    • Contribute to oversight of the development of Statistical Analysis Plans (SAPs) and statistical outputs (Analysis Data Model [ADaM], tables, figures, listings [TFLs]) generated by external vendors, ensuring that the output is consistent with analysis described in the SAP
    • Contribute to statistical analyses to answer translational questions
    • Provide support across all data analysis/ biostatistics tasks during the lifecycle of our programs from clinical study protocol design to clinical study report
    • Statistical support of exploratory biomarker studies and analysis as needed
    • Provide study data and respective text passages for clinical and regulatory study documents or publications

Your profile

  • You hold a bachelor&amp;rsquo;s or master&amp;rsquo;s degree in a Clinical or Bioscience related field, or a comparable education, ideally with a focus or first experience in statistical programming and/ or biostatistics
  • You have knowledge or deep interest in clinical data analysis, statistical programming and biostatistics, ideally you have already some industry experience in reporting and analyzing clinical trial data
  • You exhibit a great attention to detail and you have a strong affinity to answer scientific questions
  • Insights into programming in SAS, SQL, R and good programming practice are an advantage
  • Ideally, you worked already with eCRFs and gained knowledge of applicable clinical research regulatory requirements (i.e. GCP, ICH guidelines)
  • Some knowledge in immunology, oncology and/ or cell and gene therapy would be an advantage
